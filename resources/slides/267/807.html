LEAVES.

Part of a leaf.

Functions of each part.

Leaf stalk (Petiole):  To supply water to the leaf from the branch/stem.

Leaf base: Fixes the leaf on the stem.

Midrib/mid vein: Transports water and nutrients from the leaf stalk.

Veins:

  1. Supply water and minerals from the mid vein to all parts of the leaf.
  2. Collect manufactured food from all parts of the leaf to the mid vein.

Stomata:

  1. For breathing.
  2. For transpiration.

Lamina (leaf blade):

  1. For respiration.
  2. For making food / photosynthesis.

Leaf venation.

This is the arrangement of veins in the leaf.

Types of leaf venation.

  1. Network leaf venation.
  2. Parallel leaf venation.

Parallel leaf venation.

Example of plants with parallel venation.

  1. All cereals such as maize, millet, rice, sorghum
  2. Grass
  3. Sugar cane.

Network leaf venation.

Examples of plants with network venation.

  1. All legumes such as beans, peas, soya beans, ground nuts
  2. Jack fruit
  3. Mango plant.

Types of leaves.

  1. Simple leaves
  2. Compound leaves
