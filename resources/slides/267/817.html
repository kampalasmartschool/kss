Kinds of roots.

a) Prop roots.

They are commonly found on cereals.

They give extra support to plant during flowering stage (structure of prop roots).

Examples of plants with prop roots.: Maize, Ficus.

b) Adventitious roots.

These are roots which do not develop from radical but grow on any other part of a plant.

Examples:

Onion roots, Banana roots, pumpkin roots, pineapple roots.

c) Breathing roots.

Uses of roots to man.

1. Some roots are eaten.

2. Some roots are used for making herbal medicine.

3. Some roots are source of income.

4. Some roots have nodules which store nitrogen fixing bacteria that adds nitrates in the soil.

Uses of roots to plants.

1. They hold the plant firmly in the soil.

2. They suck water and mineral salts from the soil.

3. Some roots are used for breathe.

4. Some roots store food for the plant e.g. cassava, sweet potatoes.
