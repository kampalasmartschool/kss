Types of crops grown.

1. Cereals crops (grain crops).

Examples of Cereals.

  • Sorghum.
  • Millet.
  • Rice.
  • Wheat.
  • Maize.

2. Fruits.

Examples of fruits.

  • Apples.
  • Oranges.
  • Mangoes.
  • Pawpaw.
  • Pineapples.
  • Guava.

3. Vegetables.

Examples of Vegetables.

  • Cabbages.
  • Cucumber.
  • Spinach.
  • Lettuce.

4. Root crops.

Examples of Root crops.

  • Cassava.
  • Sweet potatoes.
  • Carrots.

5. Legumes (leguminous crops).

These are crops which have root nodules on the roots and store their seeds in pods.

Examples of Legumes.

  • Beans.
  • Peas.
  • Ground nuts.
  • Soya beans.

Diagram of a Legume root system.

NOTE:

  • Root nodules store Nitrogen fixing bacteria.
  • Nitrogen fixing bacteria trap Nitrogen from air in the soil and turn into nitrates.
  • Nitrates is used by plant as plant food.
  • Plants also use nitrogen and starch to make plant proteins.
