Caring of seedlings or plants.

Transplanting is the transfer of seedlings from a nursery bed to a well prepared garden.

A seedling is a young plant in a nursery bed.

Pruning is the cutting off of excess branches on a plant. E.g oranges, lemons, coffee, cocoa, tea, bananas, guava, etc.

Tool used for pruning.

Advantages of Pruning.

  • Pruning reduces the rate of transpiration.
  • Reduces competition for sunlight and air.
  • Reduces overweight and over crowdedness of the plant.

Thinning.

This is the removal of excess and poor growing seedlings in a nursery bed and plants in the garden.

Advantages of Thinning.

  1. Reduced competition for nutrients from the soil.
  2. Reduces hiding places for pests.
  3. It allows easy testing and pest control.

Plant training or staking.

This is the provision of support with a stick frame to a plant to grow up right.

Plants which are staked include: tomatoes, passion fruits.

Advantages of Plant staking.

Allows easy spray and pruning.

Allows easy harvesting of cops and weeding.

All the parts of the plants receive enough light.
