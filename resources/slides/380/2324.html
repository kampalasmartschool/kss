POEM

Read the poem and answer questions that follow in full sentences.

The abattoir is,

Like a battle field,

Filled with blood every morning,

From the innocent animals,

Sentenced to death by the butcher,

Cows, bulls, goats, and sheep,

Are neither pardoned

 

The cows moo and bulls bellow.

The goats and sheep bleat,

But no one comes to their rescue,

Because they will make a stew,

And tasty meals.

 

The customers wait for their meat,

At the butcher’s shop or stall,

The butcher cut the meat into pieces with a panga,

With the weighing scale,

Kilograms are measured,

But the vegetarian have mercy,

O they never taste the meat.
