Objectives of the EAC.

  • To create a wider market for goods from member states.
  • To promote peace, unity and cooperation.
  • To equally share the services of former East African Common Services organization.
  • To establish similar currency value by validating each country’s currency.
  • To promote transport and communication.
  • To establish similar tariffs, customs among the member states.

Organs of the East African Community.

1. The Secretariat (Arusha).

  • It was responsible for daily running of the community.
  • It was headed by the secretary general.
  • The first and last secretary was the Biginvenkya from Uganda.

2. The East African Legislative Assembly (Arusha).

  • It had 36 members.
  • Each country was represented by 9 members elected from the home parliament.
  • It acted as a law making body.

3. The Authority.

  • It was made up of the 3 heads of states from member states.
  • It was charged with general direction and control of the community affairs.

4. The East African court of appeal.

  • It was the highest legal organ.
  • It was responsible for hearing appeals from courts in 3 East African states.

5. The committee of minister.

Each member state had 1 minister and a representative to this committee.

Services provided by the East African Community.

1. The East African development bank

  • Its aim was to provide financial and technical help to member states
  • To promote balanced industrial growth through loans.
  • Its headquarters were in Kampala.

2. East African posts and telecommunication. Its headquarters were in Kampala.

3. East African railways and harbours (Mombasa)

4. The East African Examinations Council

5. The East African school of librarianship (MUK)

6. East African harbours cooperation ( Dar-es-salaam)

7. East African literature bureau, metrological department and income tax (Nairobi)

8. Virus research institution (Entebbe)

9. Fresh water fisheries research (Jinja)

10. East African civil aviation authority (Soroti )

11. East African trypanasomiasis research (Muguga- Kenya)

Note: When the East African Community collapsed in 1977 all the above collapsed except;

  • East African Development Bank
  • East African School of Librarianship
  • East African Civil Aviation Authority
