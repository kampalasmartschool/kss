Using Unless

Unless means “if not”. This is another way of expressing conditions in a negative form.

When a sentence begins with unless, a comma is used in the middle. When unless is placed in the middle, there is no comma.

When there is no negative word in both the “if clause” and “main clause,” the main clause is changed to negative.

Examples

1. If Mr. Katana gives us a motion, we shall have a debate.

Unless Mr. Katana gives us a motion, we shall not have a debate.

We shall not have a debate unless Mr. Katana gives us a motion.

2. If they miss the lesson, the teacher will punish them.

Unless they miss the lesson, the teacher won’t punish them.

The teacher won’t punish them unless they miss the lesson.

3. If she doesn’t work hard, she will not go to senior one.

Unless she works hard, she won’t go to senior one.

She won’t go to senior one unless she works hard.
