THE ACTIVE AND PASSIVE VOICE OF THE PRESENT PERFECT TENSE

An active voice describes a sentence where the action is stated by the verb. In passive voice sentences, the subject is acted upon by the verb.

Examples

  • Active:      Harry has eaten six doughnuts at dinner.

      Passive:     Six doughnuts have been eaten by Harry.

  • Active:      Sue has changed the flat tyre.

      Passive:     The flat tyre has been changed by Sue.
