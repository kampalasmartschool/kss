THE PAST CONTINUOUS TENSE

When used;

  • To show that two actions were taking place in the past at the same time.

Example

My father was mending the tyre while my mother was sweeping the kitchen.

  • To show that an activity was going on at some time in the past.

Example

Musoke was peeling matooke all morning

  • To show an activity was going on when another one happened (interruption)

Example

I was washing my clothes when he broke the cup.
