DIRECT AND INDIRECT IN PRESENT CONTINUOUS TENSE

When changing direct speech in present continuous tense, the present continuous tense changes to past continuous tense.

Direct                      Indirect

Now                                         then

Am                                           was

Is                                              was

Are                                           were

Coming                                     going

Examples

  1. Direct:        “Peter is sweeping the floor,” said Maria.
  2. Indirect       Marie said that Peter was sweeping the floor.
  3. Direct:        The headmaster said, “ I am coming to school now.
  4. Indirect      The headmaster said that he was going to school then.

NB: If a word is already given in its changed form, you leave it as it is.

Example

Indirect     Direct

Take              take

Go                  go

There            there

That               that
