DIRECT AND INDIRECT SPEECH IN PRESENT PERFECT TENSE

When changing sentences in to indirect speech, present perfect tense changes to past perfect tense.

Examples

  1. Direct:        The teacher said,” I have spoken to you about your dressing.”
  2. Indirect      The teacher said that he had spoken to me about my dressing.
  3. Direct:        He said, “I have drunk this water already.”
  4. Indirect      He says that he has drunk the water already.
