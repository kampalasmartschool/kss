STRUCTURES

USING: rather …….than

Rather than indicates a parallel structure in which two things are compared. The two things being compared need to be equal, meaning they have the same grammatical structure or form. E.g. He enjoys driving rather than walking. In this example ‘driving’ is being compared to ‘walking’, both of which are gerunds.

Examples

1. She should read her books. She is just playing.

She would rather read her books than playing

2. I had better visit my aunt than remain at home.

I would rather visit my aunt than remain at home.
