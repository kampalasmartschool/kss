CONTENT: IF III

It is used when the chance of the action to happen are over. In If 3, we just imagine what would have happened if a certain condition had been fulfilled.

NB: We use the past perfect tense n the if clause and would/could/might/should plus a past participle verb in the main clause.

Example

1. My brother being a teacher, he is teaching well.

If my brother had been a teacher, he would have taught well.

2. Kato coming early, he would catch the bus.

If Kato had come early, he would have caught the bus.

3. We organising a debate, Tom being the chairperson.

If we had organized the debate, Tom would have been the chairperson.
