Transmission of light (how light travels).

  • Light travels in straight lines to all directions from the source.

Experiments to show that light travels in straight lines.

Light travels in a straight line in any transparent medium e.g. glass, air, water and vacuum.

We cannot see around corners behind corners because light travels in straight lines.

Experiment one.

  • The experiment above shows that light travels in straight lines.
  • The three card boards have holes in their centre at exactly the same position.
  • If arranged in a straight line, light travels through the holes from the candle to eye.
  • When you shift one of the candles slightly, you will not see the light.

Experiment two.

  • In (i) when the tube is straight light can be seen.
  • In (ii) when the tube is bent, light can’t be seen.
  • This is why we can’t see around corners.

NB:

We hear sound around corners because sound travels in waves but we can’t see around corners because light travels in straight lines.
