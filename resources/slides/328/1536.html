Question tags

A question tag normally comes at the end of a sentence or statement. We use a question tag at the end of a sentence or statement in order to change it into a question and express politeness.

Note:

1. A question tag may be made up of the following helping verbs; do, does, can, will, shall, were, did, have, etc.

2. A question tag always consists of a pronoun e.g. I, she, he, you, they, it we, etc.

3. We use a negative question tag after n affirmative or positive statement.

Examples

  1. Lucy will spend her holidays here, won’t she?
  2. We shall pass the examinations, shan’t we?

4. A positive question tag is used after a negative statement.

Examples

  1. Lucy will not spend her holidays here, will she?
  2. We shall not pass the examinations, shall we?

5. After let’s………the question tag is ………..shall we?

     Examples

  1. Let us go for holidays, shall we?
  2. Let’s help our parents, shall we?

6. In requests and after the imperative do/don’t, etc the tag is usually ………will you.

     Examples

  1. Revise you books, will you?
  2. Listen to the announcement, will you?
