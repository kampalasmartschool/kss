Participle

A participle is a word formed from a verb. It can act as the main verb in a verb phrase or as an adjective to explain a noun or pronoun.

A past participle is formed by adding ‘d’ or ‘ed’ to a regular verb. E.g. cook – cooked, bake – baked.

A helping verb is usually used with the participle such as has, had, have

Example

Taj has visited several places in the city.

The exciting moments made him delay at the hotel.

ACTIVITY

Complete the following sentences using the past participle form of the verb given in bracket.

  1. I haven’t ______________________ my grandmother since last holidays. (see)
  2. By middy, the water in the fridge had _________________________. (freeze)
  3. All my clothes were _______________________ during the holidays. (tear)
  4. Have you ever __________________________ your grand parents? (visit)
  5. The village choir has _______________________ a nice song. (sing)
  6. The new term has _______________________ very well. (begin)
  7. By the time we went to bed, grandmother had ___________________ us an interesting story. (tell)
