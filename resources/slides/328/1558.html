Collective nouns

A collective noun is a name given to a number of persons or things taken together and spoken of as a whole

            Example

  • A flock of sheep
  • A class of pupils
  • A library of books
  • A congregation of worshippers
  • A company of actors
  • A host of angels
  • A sheaf of arrows
  • A band of musicians
  • A troop of soldiers
  • A choir of singers
  • A gang of thieves
  • A congregation of worshippers
  • A hail of fire
  • A swarm of bees
  • A brood of chicken
  • An audience of listeners
  • A crew of sailors
  • A staff of servants
  • A troupe of dancers
  • A crowd of people
  • A bench of judges/bishops
  • A board of directors
  • An anthology of poems
  • A bunting of flags
  • A chest of drawers
  • A fleet of ships
  • A collection of pictures
  • A forest of trees
  • A truss of hay
  • A skein of woolen thread

N.B.Collective nouns generally (but not always) take a singular verb.

Example

The herd of cattle has crossed the river.

This bouquet of flowers is expensive.
