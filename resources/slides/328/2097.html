Nouns which have two parts forming a pair are used only in the plural form.

Example

Singular                              plural

a pair of shorts                  pairs of shorts

a pair of scissors               pairs of scissors

a pair of spectacles           pairs of spectacles

a pair of compasses          pairs o compasses

a pair of shears                 pairs of shears

a pair of pants                   pairs of pants

a pair of knickers              pairs of knickers

a pair of trousers              pairs of trousers

a pair of glasses               pairs of glasses

a pair of pliers                   pairs of pliers

Many nouns taken from foreign languages keep their original plural form, whereas others take two forms

Example

Singular                    plural

formular                  formulae/formulars

stadium                   stadia/stadiums

syllabus                  syllabi/syllabuses

focus                      foci/focuses

aquarium                aquaria

radius                      radii

stimulus                  stimuli

bacterium                bacteria

larva                         larvae

pupa                        pupae

fungus                     fungi

agendum                 agenda

Some nouns have two forms for the plural, each with a different meaning.

Examples

1. Brother -        brothers:  sons of the same parent

                     Brethren:   members of a society

2. Die-              dies: stamps for coining

                     Dice: small cubes for playing games

3. Index-           indexes: tables of content in books

                     Indices: signs used in algebra

4. Cloth-            cloths: pieces of cloth

NB: Clothes refer to garments and ever in plural.
