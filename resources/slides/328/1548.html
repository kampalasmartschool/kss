COMMA

Usage of commas

1. Used to separate words in a list, phrases or clauses 

Example

He plays football, tennis, volleyball and hockey

A tall ugly man entered the room

He entered the library, walked to the history section, picked a book and started reading.

2. Used after participle phrases

Example

Chained to the post, the dog was unable to attack the thieves

Having said her prayers, the old woman lay down.

3. Used after an adverbial clause in a main clause follows;

Example

Although he worked for the company for many years, he was not promoted.

4. Used to set off words which come after a proper noun and explain or describe it

Example

George Kakoma, who composed the National Anthem, visited our school.

5. Used after Yes, No or well, if other words are to follow in a sentence

Example

Yes, he does smoke a lot

No, I don’t think so

Well, I will see what I can do for her.

6. Used with words used to address a person

Example

Mr. Mukasa, you can have a seat

Good morning, Mr. Mukasa

Come here, Jane, and I tell you

7. Used to separate items of the date

Example

Saturday, 14th May, 2012

8. Used to separate lines within an address

Example

Kinderkare Elementary School,

P. O. Box 28589,

Kampala.

9. To separate a question tag from the rest of the sentence e.g.

It is quite expensive, isn’t it?

10. Used with direct speech to mark off actual words from the reporting verb.

Example

‘I am nine years old,” said Julius
