If 3 conditional

If 3 is used to express what would have happened if a certain condition had been fulfilled.

If clause – past perfect tense

If clause

Main clause

If + had + a past participle verb

 

 

 

Example

If I had seen him in the holidays,

If we had gone to the village,

Would have + a past participle verb

Should have + a past participle verb

Could have + a past participle verb

Might have + a past participle verb

 

he would have given me some money.

we would have seen our grand parents.

 

Main clause – would / wouldn’t have e.g. If I had seen him in the holiday, he would have given me some money.

Note:

You can also express If 3 by beginning with “Had”

Example

  • Had I seen him during the holiday, he would have given me some money.
  • Had we gone to the village, we would have seen our grand parents.

When the main clause is in negative form, we use the grammatical order below.

  • Would not have
  • Cold not have
  • Should not have
  • Might not have

Example

  • If Mary had helped her mother, they would not have punished her.
  • If they had boarded the first bus, the might not have reached late.
