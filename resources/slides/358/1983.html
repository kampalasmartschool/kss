CLASSIFICATION OF FLOWERING AND NON FLOWERING PLANTS.

PLANT KINGDOM.

Plants are divided into two groups viz:

  1. Non flowering plants.
  2. Flowering plants.

a. NON – FLOWERING PLANTS.

These are plants which don’t bear flowers.

Groups of non flowering plants.

  • Spore bearing plants.
  • Coniferous plants.

i. Spore bearing plants.

These are plants that reproduce by means of spores.

They include:

  • Ferns.
  • Mosses.
  • Liverworts.
  • Horsetails.

A spore is a single cell able to develop into a new plant. A spore is protected by a spore case.

FERNS.

  • Have chlorophyll and can make their own food.
  • Have proper roots, stems and leaves.
  • They reproduce by means of spores, spores are produced at the lower side of the leaves in spore cases.
  • They grow in shady moist places.

MOSSES

  • These are small green plants that grow on house roofs, verandah, free trunks, logs, rocks, and soil in damp shady places.
  • They produce by means of spores.
  • They contain chlorophyll and make their own food.
