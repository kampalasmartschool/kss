iii. Pomes.

These are fruits in which the receptacle becomes juicy and modified as a fruit while the inner core is the pericarp.

Examples of Pomes.

  • Apples.
  • Figs.
  • Pears etc.

FRUIT AND SEED DISPERSAL.

This is the scattering of seeds and fruits from parent plants to a new environment.

Importance of seed dispersal.

  • Prevents over crowding.
  • Reduces competition for light and nutrients.
  • Enables plants to colonise new areas.
  • The farmer gets new species of crops.

Agents of seed dispersal.

  • Water.
  • Animals.
  • Wind.
  • Explosive mechanism.

Disperal by wind.

  • Seeds have wing like structures.
  • Seeds are light and easily carried away by wind.

​​​​​​​Disperal by water.

  • Seeds have air shells and can float on water for example coconuts.

Disperal by animals.

  • Fruits are edible and then the seeds deposited by the animals.
  • Some seeds like black jack stick onto the bodies of animals until they are dispersed.

Disperal by exploding.

  • Fruits have a lining on their pericarp along which it splits when it dries and hardens for example beans and peas.
