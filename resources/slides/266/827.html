SEEDS.

A seed is a fertilized ovule

Classes / Types / Groups of seeds.

  1. Monocotyledonous / monocot seeds
  2. Dicotyledonous / Dicot seeds.

Monocotyledonous seeds.

These are seeds with one cotyledon.

Examples of monocot seeds.

  • Maize
  • Wheat
  • Rice
  • Millet
  • Sorghum

NB: These seeds are also called cereals or grains.

Maize grain (fruit).

  • A maize grain is not called a true seed but it is called a fruit.
  • It is called a fruit because it has two scars.

Scars of a maize grain.

  1. Style scar.
  2. Stalk scar.
