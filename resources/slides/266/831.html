SEED GERMINATION.

Germination is the growing of a seed into a seedling.

A seedling is a young plant.

Conditions for germination.

  1. Water
  2. Warmth
  3. Oxygen

Importance of each conditions.

Water:           It softens the testa for the embryo to pass.

Oxygen :        It is used for respiration.

Warmth:          Provides the right temperature for germination.

The process of germination.

  1. Water enters the seed through the micropyle.
  2. The testa softens, relaxes and allows the embryo to pass through.
  3. The cotyledon can either remain in the ground or come out of the ground.
