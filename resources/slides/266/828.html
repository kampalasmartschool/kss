The internal parts of a maize grain/fruit.

Functions of each part.

Testa (Seed coat): Protects the inside parts of the grain.

Radicle:            Develops into root system.

Plumule:           Develops into shoot system.

Embryo:            Grows into a new plant.

Endosperm:      It stores food for the embryo.

Cotyledon:        It absorbs food from the endosperm to the embryo.

                         Supplies the food for the embryo during germination.

Style scar:        The part where the style was attached.

Stalk scar:        Attaches the grain to cob.
