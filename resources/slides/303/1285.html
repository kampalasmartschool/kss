BARTER TRADE.

  • Barter trade is the exchange of goods for services without the use of money.
  • Early traders in East Africa used barter trade.

ADVANTAGES OF BARTER TRADE.

  • Cheap.
  • Does not involve use of money.

DISADVANTAGES OF BARTER TRADE.

  • Involves cheating.
  • Divisibility was a problem.
  • Difficult to determine the value and quality of goods.
  • Double coincidence of wants/goods.

SLAVE TRADE IN EAST AFRICA.

  • Buying and selling of human beings.
  • This trade was brought to East Africa by the Arabs.
  • Slavery is the possession of a person by another person.

TRIBES INVOLVED IN SLAVE TRADE.

  1. Yao.
  2. Nyamwenzi.
  3. Baganda.
  4. Hehe.
  5. Kamba.

WHY SLAVES WERE TAKEN.

  1. To work as domestic workers.
  2. To work on plantations.
  3. To work on mines.

PLACES WHERE SLAVES WERE TAKEN.

  1. France.
  2. Britain.
  3. Holland.
  4. Portugal.
  5. Spain.
  6. America.
  7. India.
