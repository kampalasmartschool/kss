THE TEMBUZI DYNASTY IN UGANDA.

  • This was the empire to exist in the interlacustrine region of East Africa.
  • The word interlacustrine means the region of the great lakes ie around L.kyoga, L.Victoria, L.Albert and Tanganyika.
  • This region was extending from Uganda, Tanzania, Western Kenya, Democratic Republic of Congo, Rwanda and Burundi.
  • Bunyoro Kitara Empire is believed to have been founded by the Tembuzi.
  • These were the fore parents of the Chwezi people.

The hierachy of Tembuzi Dynasty. [In order]

  1. Ruhanga (founder of the Tembuzi dynasty).
  2. Nkya.
  3. Kakama Twale.
  4. Baba.
  5. Mukonko.
  6. Ngonzaki.
  7. Isaza.
  • Tembuzi dynasty contained the Tembuzi people who are believed to be semi-gods, just because they could perform miracles, they could appear and disappear according to legends.
  • Ruhanga was the founder of the Tembuzi dynasty. The name Ruhanga in this case meant God.
  • The  story  of the Tembuzi people is believed  to be a myth and this type of history  is referred to as Mythology.
  • The Tembuzi Empire existed for four regimes and their last King was Isaza.
  • When Isaza was locked underground by Namiyonga, the Tembuzi  people  disappeared  and went back to heaven.
  • The Bachwezi  became the rulers of the interlacustrine region and they were  founded by Ndahura, their first King and the grandson of  the  Isaza.
  • The last ruler of the Chwezi was King Wamala.
