THE KINGDOM OF THE BUNYORO.

  • At the beginning of the 16th century, the Luo migrated from the present day South Sudan to Uganda. They entered Bunyoro Kingdom and adopted the new dynasty referred to as Biito dynasty.
  • The founder of the Biito dynasty was Isingoma Rukidi Mpuga, son of Kyomya and he was a twin brother of Kato Kimera.
  • A separate Biito dynasty was established in Toro in the 19th Century by Prince Kaboyo and covered much of modern Buganda, Toro and Ankole.
  • Kamurasi was one of the kings of this kingdom and he was the father of the greatest King of Bunyoro called Omukama Kabalega.

    

POLITICAL ORGANISATION OF BUNYORO KINGDOM.

  • The political organisation of Bunyoro ensured order and administration from the highest to the lowest organ.
  • The title given to the king was Omukama. He appointed his prime minister and chiefs to ensure administration to all the local areas.
  • Cattle keeping and rustling was their major occupation including salt mining.
  • They exchanged salt, potatoes, wines, and iron tools for banana and bark cloth Buganda using barter trade systems.

Bunyoro weakened due to a number of factors e.g.

  • Internal local conflicts.
  • Expansion of the kingdom.
  • Internal conflicts.
  • The growth and expansion of Buganda kingdom.

ECONOMIC ORGANISATION OF BUNYORO KINGDOM.

  • The Banyoro were mainly pastoralists but Biito Bairu were crop farmers.
  • They also participated in long distance trade exchanging Ivory with beads  guns and cloth.

NOTE. The current Omukama of Bunyoro is Omukama Gafabusa Iguru.
