Urinary system.

Is made up of organs that eliminate wastes from the body in form of urine.

Other organs of urinary system.

  • Kidney.
  • Ureter.
  • Urinary bladder.
  • Urethra.

Structure of urinary system.

THE KIDNEYS.

Kidneys are two brown bean shaped organs at the back of the abdominal cavity.

Illustration of the internal structure of the kidney.

Parts of the kidney and their functions.

i. Renal artery: Is a branch of aorta that supply oxygenated blood to the kidney.

ii. Renal vein: Takes deoxygenated blood from the kidney to the vena cava.

iii. Cortex: Blood is filtered to remove Urea, Uric acid, excess salts and water.

iv. Medulla – Is a region where selective re-absorption takes place by the nephrons.

v. Pelvis- Urine is collected here from the numerous nephrons.

vi. Urethra: is a passage of urine to the Urinary bladder.

    NB: Urine is formed through ultra-filtration and selective re-absorption in the kidney

vii. The Urinary bladder: Is an elastic and muscular sack that stores urine briefly.

Diseases of the kidney.

  1. Cancer of the kidney.
  2. Kidney failure.
  3. Kidney stones.
  4. Bilharziasis

Waste products excreted by the kidney.

  • Uric acids.
  • Urea.
  • Excess salts.
  • Excess water.
