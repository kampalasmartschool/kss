MULTIPLES.

A multiple is a product of a given number and another whole greater than zero e.g.

4 x 2  =  8,  and  8 is a multiple of 4.

(i)         List multiples of 4                                            

1  x  4  =  4

2  x  4  =  8

3  x  4  =  12

4  x  4  =  16       

5  x  4  =  20

6  x  4  =  24

(ii)        List multiples of 5

1  x  5   =   5

2  x  5   =   10

3  x  5   =  15

4  x  5   =  20

5  x  5   =  25

6  x  5   =  30

{4,  8,  12,  20,  24, ___ }

{5,  10,  15,  20,  25,  30,  ___ }

COMMON MULTIPLES AND L.C.M.

Examples.

Find the first common multiples of 2 and 4

Common multiples = {4, 8, 12, 16}

2. Find the L.C.M of 4 and 5

      M4 = {4,   8,   12,   16,   20,   24,   28}

      M5 = {5, 10, 15, 20, 25,  30, ......}

    Common multiples = { 20}

  L.C.M is 20.
