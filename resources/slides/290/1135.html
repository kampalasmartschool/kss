Nouns that end in “Y”  make their plurals by changing  “y” into “ies”  ​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​

baby                  babies

army                  armies

factory             factories

lady                 ladies

city                   cities

party                parties

fly                    flies

body                  bodies

There are one or two nouns that don’t follow any of these rules.

Common nouns that end with ‘y’ preceded by a vowel letter simply take ‘s’ in their plural. ​​​​​​​

donkey             donkeys

valley               valleys

tray                 trays

key                  keys ​​​​​​​

turkey             turkeys

day                  days

spray                sprays

trolley              trolleys

boy                 boys

toy                   toys

railway            railways ​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​
