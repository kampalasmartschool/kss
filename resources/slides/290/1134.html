COMMON NOUNS 

Common nouns are used to name only one class, people, and place or things e.g. girl, tree, town , etc

They are sub – divided in singular and plural, countable and un countable nouns.  Nouns that mean “only one” are singular.  Nouns that mean “more than one” are plural.

  • most nouns form their plural by adding  “s”   e.g. animals, houses , dogs, books, spaces  etc
  • Some nouns form their plurals by adding “ es”

Nouns that end in a hissing sound e.g.

​​​​​​​​​​dress                            dresses

box                               boxes

torch                             torches

brush                            brushes

church                          churches

branch                         branches

inch                              inches

Some nouns that end in ‘o’ add ‘es’

potato                   potatoes

mosquito              mosquitoes

hero                      heroes

cargo                     cargoes

​​​​​​​Other nouns that end in ‘o’ add only ‘s’

photos, pianos, dynamos, radios, studios, videos, bamboos, igloos, eskimos

Nouns that end in “f”  or  “fe”  form their plurals by changing  “f”   or  “fe”  to “ves” 

leaf  - leaves                  hoof   - hooves                 wife   -  wives                   thief  -  thieves 

half  -  halves                 wolf  -  wolves                 knife   -  knives     

Exceptional

chiefs, hoofs, roofs, gulfs, staffs, beliefs, stuffs, handkerchiefs
