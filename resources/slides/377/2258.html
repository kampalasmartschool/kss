                                A VISIT TO A CARPENTRY WORKSHOP

Carpenter:        I am pleased to see you, dear pupils of Kinderkare Elementary School.

Pupils:             Thank you! We have great pleasure of meeting you.

Carpenter:        Where is the teacher who has accompanied you?

Pupils:             He is still in the school bus. He is Mr. Emaru, our teacher of English in P.6.

Carpenter:        Would you like ask any questions, please?

Pupils:             Certainly, Mr. Kisosso. We have just learnt about carpentry. What is a plane used for?

Carpenter:        To make the wood smooth.

Pupils:             Thank you Mr. Kisosso. What do we need to make a chair?

Carpenter:        You need some wood, a plane, a saw, some nails and vanish.

Pupils:             That is wonderful! What steps do you take to make a chair?

Carpenter:        First, you plane a wood, next you cut it into pieces, and then you fix the pieces firmly together using nails and a hammer.

Pupils:             Mr. Kisosso, what do you do when you want to polish a chair or any other piece of furniture?

Carpenter:        First, you make it smooth with sand paper, next you polish it using vanish and a brush, and then you put the chair in the sun to dry.

Pupils:             Dear sir, will you please tell us why you apply vanish to wooden objects?

Carpenter:        Well, we polish wooden objects with vanish to make them shiny and good looking.

Pupils:             We didn’t know all this information about carpentry. Thank you very much Mr. Kisosso. Good bye.
