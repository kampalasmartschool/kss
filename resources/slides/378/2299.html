DIALOGUE

Read the dialogue and answer the questions in full sentences.

      AT THE TAILOR’S WORK PLACE

Baba:   What are you cutting with a pair of scissors, Katana?

Tailor: I am cutting a piece of cloth.

Baba:   What are you going to sew?

Tailor:  I am going to sew a suit for Mr. Bumba.

Baba:   By the way, what did you specialize in?

Tailor:  I specialized in sewing ladies’, children’s and men’s suits.

Baba:   What is that long narrow strip of plastic on your shoulder?

Tailor:  It is a tape measure. I use it for measuring the length of cloth and my customers before I make them clothes.

Baba:   Do you mind patching my torn pair of trousers first?

Tailor:  No, I don’t. What tore it?

Baba:   A nail. How much will you charge me for patching it?

Tailor:  One thousand five hundred shillings only. But wait a minute. My needle has broken. Let me grease my sewing machine and wear a thimble on my thumb before I replace the needle to avoid being pierced of hurt.
