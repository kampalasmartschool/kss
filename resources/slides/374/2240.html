Using: if…./ if….(if 1)

If sentences are also called conditional sentences because in them, there are conditions.

If 1 is used to express something that is likely to happen when a certain condition is fulfilled.

We use the present simple tense in the “If” clause and future simple tense in the main clause.

When if begins a sentence, a comma is used in the middle. When the main clause begins a sentence, no comma is needed.

Examples

  1. If you give a point of order, I will stop talking and listen.
  2. You will hear what the speakers say if you listen carefully.
  3. If they give more points, they will win the debate.
  4. The teacher will punish us if we don’t participate in the debate.
