INVERTEBRATES.

These are animals without a back bone.

They are multicellular animals.

Classes of invertebrates.

  • Coelenterates.
  • Echinoderms
  • Sponges.
  • Worms.
  • Molluscs.
  • Arthropods.

a) Coelenterates.

  • They have cylindrical bodies with only one opening which acts as the mouth and the anus.
  • They have stinging cells on the tentacles surrounding the openings.
  • They live in water.

Examples of coelenterates.

  • Corals.
  • Jelly fish.
  • Hydra.
  • Sea anemone.

 Jelly fish

b) Echinoderms.

The have spiny skins and tube feet.

They pump liquid into their feet to make them expand.

Examples of Echinoderms.

  • Star fish.
  • Sea urchins.
  • Sea lilies.
  • Sea cucumbers.

​​​​​​​ Star fish.

c) Sponges.

  • Sponges are a collection of individual cells organized into one body on the sea floor.
  • They cannot move about but remain stuck on the sea floor.
  • They suck in sea water and filter out tiny pieces of food.
  • They breathe and feed through the holes in their bodies.
