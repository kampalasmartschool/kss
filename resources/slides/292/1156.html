PHYSICAL FEATURES IN UGANDA.

Physical features are land forms of an area.

Relief is the general appearance of the landscape

Classification of Physical Features.

  • Relief features e.g. mountains, valleys, plains, hills etc.
  • Drainage features are aground water systems like lakes, rivers, oceans and seas.

Examples of major physical features in Uganda.

  • Mountains.

  • Lakes.

  • Rivers.

  • Rift valley.

  • Plateau.

1. Plateau.

A plateau can also be called a table land.

A plateau is a flat raised topped piece of land.

The plateau covers the largest part of Uganda.

Activities carried out on plateau.

  • Farming.
  • Mining.
  • Industrialization.
  • Lumbering.
  • Tourism.

Importance of  a plateau.

  • They have fertile soil.
  • They are sources of minerals.
  • They receive reliable rainfall.

Problems faced by people on a plateau.

  • There is soil erosion.
  • Poor transport system especially in hilly areas.
  • Crop pests and diseases.

2. Mountains / highlands.

A mountain is the highest piece of land (about 2000 metres above the sea level).

Types of mountains.

  • Block.
  • Volcanic.
  • Inselbergs.

Examples of mountains in Uganda.

  • Rwenzori.
  • Elgon.
  • Moroto.
  • Mufumbiro ranges.

a) Mountain Rwenzori.

  • It is the highest mountain in Uganda.
  • It is found in Western Uganda in the district of Kasese and Bundibugyo.
  • It is a block mountain with many peaks that form ranges.
  • It is found along the border of DRC and Uganda.
  • Its highest peak is Margherita.
  • Peak Margherita is snow rapped throughout the year because “The peak crosses the snowline”.
  • HM Stanley was the first European to see Mt. Rwenzori.
  • He named it mountains of the moon because its peaks looked like a rising moon.
  • The Bamba and Bakonjo live on its slopes in Uganda while the Pygmies live on it in DRC.
  • Donkeys are the major means of transport.
