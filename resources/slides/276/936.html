DENSITY.

Density is mass per unit volume. The density of an object is found after knowing its mass and its volume. The formula for finding density is:-

Density= Mass÷Volume.

Mass = Volume x Density

Volume = Mass ÷ Density

The units for density are grammes per cubic units.

Finding units for density.

Density= Mass÷Volume.

Units for mass are grammes (g)

Units for Volume are cubic units e.g cm3

Therefore units for Density = g÷cm3     

D  =    g/ cm3     

Other units for volume are:-

  • Grammes per cubic centimetres (g/cc)
  • Grammes per cubic mililitres g/ml3

Densities of liquids.

Liquids have different densities.

Density of liquids is measured by an instrument called a hydrometer.
