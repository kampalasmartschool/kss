ADVANTAGES OF MULCHING

  1. It controls soil erosion.
  2. Mulches decay and form humus.
  3. It controls weed growth.
  4. It moderates soil temperature.
  5. It increases water infiltration in the soil.

DISADVANTAGES OF MULCHING.

  1. Mulches can be fire hazards.
  2. Mulches may hide pests.
  3. Mulching is tiresome and expensive.
  4. Mulches may act as breeding places of pests.
  5. Mulches may be a source of seeds of weeds.

Crop Rotation.

This is the growing of different crops on the same piece of land seasonally. Crop rotation was discovered by Lord Shed Town.

FACTORS TO CONSIDER WHEN CARRYING OUT CROP ROTATION.

  • Legumes are rotated with other crops.
  • Crops in the same family should not follow each other.
  • Cover crops should be alternated with less vegetative leaf crops.
  • Deep rooted crops to rotted with shallow rooted crops.
  • Crops easy to weed to be alternated with those difficult to weed.
  • There should be resting phase.

ADVANTAGES OF CROP ROTATION.

  1. It breaks the life cycle of pests.
  2. Legumes add nutrients to soil.
  3. Parasitic weeds are controlled.
  4. It maintains soil fertility.
  5. It controls soil erosion.
  6. It improves crop yields.

Cover Cropping.

This is the growing of crops which cover the soil. Such crops are called Cover crops.

Examples of Cover crops: Pumpkins, Peas, Tomatoes, Groundnuts etc.

Inter cropping.

This is the growing of two or more crops on the same piece of land.
