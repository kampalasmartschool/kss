SOIL

Soil is the top layer of the earth.

It is the medium in which plants grow and where they get water and nutrients.

TERMS USED IN SOIL

Land reclamation: this is the bringing back waste land to use.

Drainage: this is the method of removing excess water from a water logged area.

Irrigation: this is the artificial method of providing water crops in dry areas.

Soil sampling: this is the taking of sample of soil from different parts of the field to analyse it in a laboratory.

Soil texture: this means the different sizes of soil particles in an area.

Soil structure: this is the way in which particles are arranged in the soil.

Soil profile: This is the vertical arrangement of the various soil layers.

IMPORTANCE OF SOIL

  1. It holds water for plants
  2. It contains nutrients for the growing plants
  3. It contains air which plants need
  4. It holds plants firmly into the ground
  5. It is a home to small animals

COMPONENTS OF SOIL

Soil consists of the following:

  1. Humus(organic matter).
  2. Water.
  3. Air.
  4. Particles of clay and sand.
  5. Dissolved mineral salts (in-organic matter).

Examples of minerals needed by plants.

  1. Magnesium and iron.
  2. Potassium.
  3. Calcium.
  4. Phosphorus.
  5. Nitrates and phosphates.
