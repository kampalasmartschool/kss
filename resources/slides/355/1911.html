SOIL FERTILITY.

Soil fertility is where soil contains enough water, air, humus and nutrients.

Ways of maintaining soil fertility.

  1. Practicing crop rotation.
  2. Leaving the soil to rest.
  3. Practicing mulching.
  4. Using fertilizers.

There are two groups of fertilizers namely:-

  1. Natural Fertilizers.
  2. Artificial fertilizers.

1. NATURAL FERTILIZERS.

These are organic fertilizers which are made from decayed plant and animal remains.

Farm yard manure

They include:

  1. Green manure. This is got from decayed green plants which are buried in soil.
  2. Farm yard manure. This is got from decayed and animal dung and urine.
  3. Compost manure. This is obtained from decayed household refuse like left over foods, rubbish and other plant remains.

ADVANTAGES OF NATURAL MANURE.

  1. They improve soil texture.
  2. They improve water retention in the soil.
  3. They release nutrients slowly.
  4. They stay longer in soil.
  5. They are made from plants and animals.

DISADVANTAGES OF NATURAL MANURE.

  1. They smell badly.
  2. They are tiresome to make.
  3. Plant and animal matter may not be easily got.
