Types of Soil Erosion.

They include:

i. Sheet erosion. This is when flowing water takes away the top soil and begins making shallow channels in the ground.

ii. Gully erosion.  this is when a lot soil is carried away by running water and big channels called gullies are made in the ground.

iii. Rill erosion. This is when flowing water takes away the top soil and begins making shallow channels in the ground.

iv. Wind erosion

METHODS OF CONTROLLING SOIL EROSION.

a) Terracing. This is the cutting of steps (terraces) across the slopes to the  speed of running water.

b) Contour ploughing. This is the digging of lines of contour across the slopes to slow the speed of running water.

c) Strip Cropping (Bunding). This is the growing of crops and strips of grass are left in the spaces to reduce the speed of running water.

d) Afforestration. This is the planting of trees in areas where they have never been before.

e) Reafforestration. This is the planting of trees in areas where they have never been before.

f) Bush fallowing. This is the practice of leaving the land to grow bushy for some time.

g) Mulching. This is the covering of the soil with dry plant materials

The main purpose of mulching is to keep moisture in the soil.

The materials used to cover the top soil are called mulches.

Examples of Mulches.

  1. Dry grass like spear grass, elephant grass etc.
  2. Dry banana leaves
  3. Coffee husks
  4. Maize straw
  5. Sorghum straw
  6. Wood shavings.
