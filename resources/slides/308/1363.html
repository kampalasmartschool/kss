Ox-bow lakes.

Formed as a result of river meandering and deposition.

N.B:   A meander is a curved bend of a river.

Diagrams to show the formation of ox-bow lakes.

Man-made lakes:

These are lakes which were formed by blocking a flowing stream.

Examples are: L. Kariba, L. Nasser,  L. Volta  (largest man made lake in Africa)

RIVERS OF AFRICA.

Africa has many rivers but there are two major types:

  1. Perennial rivers: These flow throughout the year.

  2. Seasonal rivers: These flow during the wet season and dry up during the dry season.

NOTE: Most of the rivers have their sources in highland areas because these areas receive reliable rainfall.

Stages of a river.

  1. Upper course / Youthful / Torrent.
  2. Middle course / mature.
  3. Lower course / old / senile.

Characteristics of Each Stage.

1. Upper course

  • Water falls and rapids.
  • Water flows very fast.
  • It has a V- shaped valleyor a Gorge.

2. Middle course

  • U-shaped valley.
  • Water flows gently.
  • Meanders start developing.

3. Lower course

  • It forms Ox-bow lakes.
  • Well developed meanders.
  • Deltas estuaries.
  • Rivers flow over flood plains due to deposition.

Importance of:-

1. Upper course

  1. Water falls help to generate Hydro Electric Power.
  2. Waterfalls and rapids attract tourists.
  3. Water sporting games e.g rafting.

2. Middle course

  • It is most suitable for Navigation.
  • It is also used for fishing.

3. Lower course

  • Agriculture due to deposited silt
