2. Volcanic lakes.

Volcanicity is the process by which molten rocks (magma) is exposed on the earth’s surface.

These are lakes formed as a result of volcanicity. They are:

  • Crater lakes:

  • Found on  dead volcanic mountain e.g L. Katwe, L. Muhavura, Ngorongoro, Nyamunuka.
  • Lava dammed lakes:

  • Formed as a result of lava blocking the drainage system e.g  L. Bunyonyi , L. Mutanda.
  • Caldera lakes –

  • An enlarged and deepened crater lakes.

3. Rift valley lakes.

These lakes are formed as a result of faulting.

These include:

L.Tanganyika, L.Malawi, L.Turkana, L.Naivasha, L.Natron, L.Eyasi, L.Baringo, L.Bogoria, L.Edward, L.Albert, L.Nakuru, L.Magadi, L.Manyara, L.Rukwa.

Characteristics of Rift valley lakes.

  1. Most of them are salty.  [Why? They don’t have outlets/They have salty rocks under them]
  2. Most of them are deep.
  3. Most of them are long (oblong).
  4. Most of them are narrow.
  5. They do not have outlets
  6. They have salty rocks underneath.
