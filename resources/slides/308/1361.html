PHYSICAL FEATURES OF AFRICA AND THEIR FORMATION.

Physical features are the landforms on the earth’s surface.

They can also be called Relief features.

Relief means the general appearance of the land.

Examples of physical features include:

Mountains, lakes and rivers, plain, valleys, plateau, highlands, coral reefs, lagoons, natural harbours.

[Review the examples of landforms in Uganda, East Africa and then Africa.]

Physical features of Africa include:

  • Coastal plain

  • Plateaus

  • Mountain ranges or highlands

  • Rift valleys

  • Lakes, rivers oceans and seas (Drainage features).

FORMATION OF PHYSICAL FEATURES.

Lakes.

A lake is a large mass of water.

Types of lakes.

1. Depression lakes (Down warped / Basin lakes).

Formation.

They were formed as a result of down warping.

Examples are: L.Victoria, L.Kyoga, L.Wamala, L.Chad, L.Opeta, L.Bisina, L.Kwania and L.Nakuwa

Characteristics of Depression lakes: 

  1. They are shallow.

  2. They usually have fresh water.

  3. They have irregular shape.

NB: These lakes have fresh water because they have both inlets and outlets.
