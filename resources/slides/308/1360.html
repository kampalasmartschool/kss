Position of Africa.

Africa is located between longitudes 17°W , 52°E and latitudes 38°N , 35°S.

Ways of locating places on a map.

  1. Using lines of longitudes and latitudes (Grid references method)

The major longitude that crosses Africa is the Greenwich Meridian (Prime Meridian) 0°.

The major latitudes that cross Africa are basically three and they are:

  1. Tropic of Cancer (23 ½°N)

  2. Equator (0°)

  3. Tropic of Capricorn (23 ½°S)

Note:

Longitudes are imaginary lines drawn on a map from North to South on the surface of the earth while latitudes are imaginary lines drawn on  a map from East to West on the surface of the earth.

  1. Longitude is the distance East or West of Greenwich Meridian.

  2. Latitude is the distance North or South of the Equator.

Importance of longitudes and latitudes.

a) Longitudes.

  1. These are used to determine time e.g. the prime meridian.
  2. The International Date line determines days and dates.

b) Latitudes.

  1. Latitudes are used to determine climate e.g. the Equator

Both longitudes and latitudes locate places on a map.

NB:

  1. Major latitudes outside Africa are:-

    1. Arctic circle.

    2. Antarctic circle.

  2. A major longitude outside Africa is the International Date Line (IDL) marked 180° East.

Other ways of locating places on a map.

  1. Using compass direction. [Review the neighbours of Uganda and East Africa using the compass direction.] 
  2. Using landmarks.
  3. Using neighbours.

N.B: The method of locating places using longitudes and latitudes is called the Grid Reference Method.
