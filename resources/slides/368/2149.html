PRESENT CONTINUOUS TENSE

The present continuous tense is used:

a) To describe what is happening at the present moment.

Examples

  • I am reading my book.
  • They are learning English.
  • He is going to Kampala.
  • The boy is playing football, etc.

b) To describe actions that began in the past and is still going on, even though the action might not be taking place at the time of speaking or writing.

Examples

  • My brother is learning to drive (he started sometimes ago and has not yet finished.
  • There is a teacher who is giving me extra lesson in mathematics.

c) For future activities.

Examples

  • We are going out this evening.
  • He is arriving on Sunday.

d) For habits but only when a word such as always and constantly is included in the sentences.

Examples

  • Jane is always rude to her parents.
  • When he drives, he is always taking risks.
  • Maria is always complaining about the services in the restaurant.

NB:

1. It is used on the verbs which do not carry the “ing” form because actions take place in our mind. These verbs do not go with ‘am’, ‘is’ and ‘are’. They are:

2. When using the above verbs with she, he or it, we add “s” or “es” to the verb.
