It was early in January this year when the PLE results 2014 were released. Many candidates became anxious because they wanted to know their results. They therefore sent SMS to 6600

While releasing the results at Uganda National Examinations Board (UNEB) Headquarters in Ntinda, the Minister of Education and Sports, Hon. Janet Kataha Meseveni was glad to announce that most of the candidates had passed the examinations.

Besides, the general report indicated that Social Studies was the best done subject. However, she also noted that Mathematics was the worst done subject. The minister congratulated all the candidates on their success. She also thanked the teachers and head teachers who had taught the candidates and conducted examinations without any malpractice.

Since urban schools performed better than rural school, the minister warned teachers in rural areas against absenteeism, late coming and high rate of school dropout in rural schools. She promised that the ministry of Education and Sports would come up with a new policy very soon.

Finally, the minister called upon head teachers of secondary schools to assemble at Mandela National Stadium at Namboole for selection of successful candidates to secondary schools of their choice.
