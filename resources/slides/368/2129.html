Use of ……….although……….

Although is used for introducing a statement that makes the main statement in a sentence seem surprising e.g. Although the sun was shining, it wasn’t warm.

It is also used to mean ‘but’ or ‘however’ when you are commenting on a statement e.g. I felt he was wrong although I didn’t say it during the meeting.

Examples

1. The teacher was sick. The children organized the debate.

Although the teacher was sick, the children organized the debate.

The children organized the debate although the teacher was sick

2. James got out. The chairperson did not give him permission.

Although the chairperson did not give James permission, he got out.

James got out although the chairperson did not give him permission.
