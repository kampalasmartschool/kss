LAKES OF EAST AFRICA.

A lake is a depression or a hallow on the earth’s surface filled with (by) water.

Examples Of Lakes.

  • L. Victoria

  • L.Tanganyika.

  • L.Kyoga.

  • L.Magadi.

  • L.Wamala.

  • L.Nabugabo.

  • L.Turkana.

Types of Lakes In East Africa.

1. Depression (basin) lakes/down warped lakes; these can also be called down warping lakes.They were formed by the process of down warping.eg.

  • L. Victoria.

  • L. Kyoga.

  • L. Kwania.

  • L. Amboseli in Kenya.

Characteristics Of Basin/Depression Lakes.

  • They have fresh waters because they have outlets.
  • They are wide.
  • They are shallow.
  • They have swampy surroundings.
  • They have irregular shape.

2. RIFT VALLEY/FAULTY LAKES.

  • They are found on the rift valley floor.
  • They were formed by the process of faulting.

Examples of Rift Valley Lakes.

  1. L.Tanganyika.
  2. L.Turkana.
  3. L.Albert.
  4. L.Edward.

Characteristics Of Rift Valley Lakes.

  • They are deep.
  • They are long and narrow.
  • They have salty water because they don’t have outlets.

Some rift valley lakes have fresh waters because they have outlets eg.L.Naivasha, L.Tanganyika, L. Albert, and .L.Malawi.
