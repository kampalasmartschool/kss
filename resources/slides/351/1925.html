HOW HEAT TRAVELS.

There are three ways how heat travels from one place to another.

These are:-

  1. Conduction.
  2. Convection.
  3. Radiation.

Conduction.

This is the process by which heat passes (travels) through solids.

In solids heat is transferred from one heated molecule to another. This transfers heat until the whole solid object is heated.

Experiment to show how heat travels by Conduction.

Convection.

This is the process by which heat travels through liquids and gases.

Heat in liquids and gases travels in form of convectional currents.

The particles which are heated become light and move upwards while the cooler and heavier particles move downwards.

Illustration.
