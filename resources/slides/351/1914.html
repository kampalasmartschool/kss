Characteristics of each state of matter.

1. Solids.

  1. Solids always have certain(definite) shapes.
  2. Some solids change into liquids by heating.
  3. Solids don’t change the form of state when cooled.
  4. Some solids expand when heated.
  5. Have molecules closely held together.
  6. The force of attraction between molecules is great (cohesion).

Molecules held together

2. Liquids.

  1. Have no definite shapes i.e take the shape of the container.
  2. Some liquids change into gases by heating.
  3. Some liquids change into solids by freezing.
  4. Liquids expand more when heated.
  5. The cohesion force is weak and that is why molecules move about freely inside the liquids.

3. Gases.

  1. Have molecules far apart.
  2. Some gases cannot be seen but can be felt.
  3. Gases have neither definite shapes nor volumes.
  4. Gases expand the most when heated.
  5. Gases cannot be touched.

Molecules are small particles that make up matter.

An atom is the smallest molecule in matter.
