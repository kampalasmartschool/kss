Solutes, solvents and solutions.

A solute:

Is a substance that can easily dissolve in a liquid.

Examples of solutes

  • Salt
  • Sugar
  • Glucose.

A solvent:

Is a liquid that dissolves a solute.

Examples of a solvent is water.

NB: Water is called a universal solvent as it dissolves all solutes.

Substances which do not dissolve in a solvent such as water, is said to insoluble.

Examples of insoluble substances:

  • Stones
  • Ash
  • Sand.

A solution

Is a substance formed after a solute has dissolved (disappeared) in a solvent.
