EFFECTS OF HEAT ON MATTER.

Heat causes temperature of matter to increase.

Heat can cause some solids to change to liquid and liquids to gases.

Heat can also cause some objects to expand (increase in size).

EXPANSION AND CONTRACTION.

Expansion is the increase in size of an object.

Most substances increase in size when heated.

Contraction is the decrease in size of an object.

Solids, liquids and gases all expand and contract at different rates.

Gases expand fastest, followed by liquids and solids expand least.

Gases expand greatest because their molecules are farther apart and free to move.
