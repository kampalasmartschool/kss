THE SIX’S MINIMUM AND MAXIMUM THERMOMETER.

This type of thermometer measures the minimum and maximum temperature of the day.

Maximum temperature is recorded during the day while minimum temperature is recorded during the night.

This thermometer is commonly found in places like:

  1. Research stations.
  2. Schools.
  3. Universities.
  4. Weather stations.

WALL THERMOMETER.

This is the type of thermometer which is hung in our houses and offices.

  • It measures temperature in that room.
  • It uses mercury.
  • Normal room temperature is 25°C.

Liquids used in thermometers.

There are two liquids used in thermometers namely:

  1. Mercury.
  2. Alcohol.

REASONS WHY MERCURY IS USED IN THERMOMETERS

  1. Mercury is easily seen.
  2. Mercury does not stick on the glass.
  3. Mercury is a good conductor of heat.
  4. Mercury does not boil easily.
  5. Mercury has even and regular expansion.

ADVANTAGES OF USING ALCOHOL OVER MERCURY.

  1. It does not  solidify easily.
  2. Alcohol expands more than mercury.

DISADVANTAGES OF USING ALCOHOL OVER MERCURY.

  • It is colourless so it not easily seen.
  • It sticks on the wall glass of the tube.
  • It is a bad conductor of heat.
  • It needs a lot of heat to expand.
  • Its expansion is not uniform.
  • It evaporates.
