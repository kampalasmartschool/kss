Radiation.

This is the process by which heat travels through vacuum.

When heat travels by radiation, it doesn’t need any medium of transmission.

Examples of radiation in nature.

  • Heat from the sun reaches us by radiation.
  • When you sit near a fire place, charcoal stove (sigiri) or cooker or even ironing clothes using a flat iron, you receive heat by radiation.

Importance of conduction.

  1. Conduction of heat enables us to iron our clothes.
  2. It helps us to cook.
  3. Enables us to roast meat.

Importance of convection.

  1. Convection currents help to drive out bad smell through vent pipe of a VIP latrine.
  2. Smoke is driven out of houses through chimneys by means of convection currents.
  3. A lit cigarette is kept burning by convection current.
  4. A Charcoal stove, flat iron and a lantern lamp continue burning by convection currents.
  5. Convection currents help us to get fresh air in our houses.
  6. Water in a kettle on fire boils by convection current.

Importance of radiation.

  1. Radiant heat from the sun helps to dry our clothes and harvested crops.
  2. Heat from the fire reaches our bodies by radiation.
  3. Radiant heat from the sun helps in rain formation.
