Types of magnets.

  • Natural magnets.
  • Artificial magnets.

a. Natural magnets.

These are magnets that exist on their own in nature without man making them.

They are:

         (a) Lodestone (Magnetite)

         (b) The earth

b. Artificial magnets.

These are magnets made by man.

They are named according to their shapes.

These include;

  1. Horse shoe magnet.
  2. Bar magnet.
  3. Needle magnet.
  4. Cylindrical magnets.
  5. Electro magnets.

TYPES OF ARTIFICIAL MAGNETS.

These are:

  1. Temporary magnets. – Are magnets which lose their magnetism easily eg electro magnet
  2. Permanent magnets.

Permanent magnets retain their magnetism for a longtime.

Examples of Permanent magnets.

Horse shoe

Bar magnet

Cylindrical magnets

Needle magnet

Advantages of temporary magnets.

  • We can use any magnetic material to make them.
  • Hysterisis loss is minimised.

Disadvantages of temporary magnets.

  • They lose their magnetism so fast.
  • They are not strong.

TERMS USED IN MAGNETISM:

  • Poles of magnets: These are the ends of a magnet.
  • Magnetic field: This is an area around a magnet where the force of magnetism is formed.
  • Magnetic lines of  force: These are lines around a magnet through which magnetism runs from North to South Pole.
