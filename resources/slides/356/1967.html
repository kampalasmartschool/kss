Properties of magnets.

a. A freely suspended magnet faces in the North – South direction.

b. Magnets are strongest at poles.

When a magnet is lowered into iron fillings, there are more filings attracted at the poles compared to along the lengt of the bar magnet.

c. Like poles of a magnet repel while unlike poles attract each other.

d. Magnetic lines of force run from North Pole to South Pole.

e. Magnetism can pass through a non-magnetic material.

f. When a magnet is broken into pieces each piece becomes an independent magnet.
