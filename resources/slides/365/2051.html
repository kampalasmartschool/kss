CULTURE

Culture is a way of life. It is the way people live, speak, work and perform traditional ceremonies such as naming children, last funeral rites, burial, marriages and installing heirs.

 

In Uganda, different tribes speak different languages. For instance in Buganda, people speak Luganda. In Mbarara, people speak Lunyakole and in Mbale people speak Lugisu or Lumasaba. In schools, children and teachers use English as a medium of communication because it is their official language.

 

In addition, different tribes have different cultures. In Bugisu, in order for a boy to become a man, he has to undergo circumcision. Circumcision is usually accompanied by Isongo or Imbalu dance.

 

In Bunyoro, there is Runyege dance. The Runyege is a social dance which expresses love. It is performed by both boys and girls.

 

In Acholi, they have the Larakaraka dance. Larakaraka is a wedding dance. In this dance, there is singing which tells a love story. At first, the boys sing a song of love when the girls are quiet. Next, the girls reply that they will marry the boys. Then both the boys and the girls form couples and dance happily to the music.
