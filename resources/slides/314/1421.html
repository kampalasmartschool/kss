CALCULATIONS ON SOUND.

NB:   The speed of sound in air is 330 metres per second.

Examples.

1.    If a man heard a gunshot after four seconds. How far was he from the firing point?

Distance    =   Speed   x Time taken

D   =  ( 330m   x   4 ) metres

D   =  1320m

2.    It took 3 seconds to hear echo of a man chopping wood. How far was the man from a chopping place?

D   = (S x T) ÷ 2

D  =  (330 x 3 ) ÷ 2 metres

D  =  990 ÷ 2m

D  = 495m

NB:  We divide by 2 because there are two sets of sound waves i.e. original waves and the reflected waves (Echo).

Echo is reflected sound.

3.   Amooti was standing across the valley, which was 660m away from the cliff if he shouts, how long will it take to hear the echo?

T   = (D÷S) x 2

T   = (660 ÷ 330) x 2

T  =  (2  x 2) seconds

      =  4  seconds.

[Again we multiply the time by 2 because of the echo. (Time for sound to reach the cliff and time for the echo to reach Amooti.)]

4.   Okello was standing 165m away from his father who called him by clapping. How long did it take him to hear the clapping?

T   = (D÷S)

T   = (165 ÷ 330)

T  =  0.5 seconds

[Here we don’t multiply by two because there’s no echo. Sound goes from the father to Okello.]
