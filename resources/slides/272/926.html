SITING THE HIVE.

This refers to the process of selecting a suitable place in which to put the hive.

Factors to Consider when siting a hive.

  • A  Place sheltered from strong sunlight and wind.
  • Presence of flowering plants around the apiary.
  • A Place  free from noise and other disturbances.
  • A Place free from bee enemies.

NB:  An apiary is a group of bee hives with bees.

STOCKING THE HIVE.

Stocking refers to putting bees into an empty hive or refers to the process of encouraging bees to occupy an empty hive.

How it is done.

  • Setting up the hive in its permanent place: Smear the inside with bee wax and wait for the bees to occupy on their own.
  • Use of a catcher box.
  • Catching a swarm with a swarm catching net.

 Fig 1: Swarm Catching Net.

Feeding Of Bees.

During drought or in shortage of food, bees can be fed on:-

  1. Ordinary sugar mixed with water to make.
  2. Ordinary water.

Harvesting Honey.

Harvesting of honey is done in the evening.

Why? All bees usually settle inside the hive in the evening.
