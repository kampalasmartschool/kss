DIALOGUE

Study the dialogue and answer questions that follow in full sentences.

Reporter:          Hello, I am pleased to meet you, Sir.

Chong Lee:      Hello, I pleased to meet you too.

Reporter:          I am Robinson, I report for New Vision newspaper.

Chong Lee:      I am Chong Lee.

Reporter:          May I ask you a few questions, Sir?

Chong Lee:      Yes, please you may.

Reporter:          Where do you come from and what is your nationality?

Chong Lee:      I come from china and I am a Chinese by nationality.

Reporter:          How do you feel being here in Uganda?

Chong Lee:      I feel really overwhelmed because I have been given a warm reception.

Reporter:          Why did you decide to come to Uganda?

Chong Lee:      I am a Chinese businessman. I therefore, decided to come to Uganda because I am an investor.

Reporter:          Which commodity are you interested in mostly?

Chong Lee:      I am interested in matchboxes.

Reporter:          Do you have enough capital to invest in Uganda, Sir?

Chong Lee:      Of course yes! Why have you asked me such a question?

Reporter:          You know! You see! Some people come here as investors but end up on the streets doing small scale businesses that can be done by the local people for instance selling chapatti, pan cakes or even making bricks.

Chong Lee:      I am not of that type

Reporter:          Have you already registered with Uganda Investment Authority?

Chong Lee:      Yes, I have. I have already been given a certificate of registration. Besides, I have been allocated a piece on of land at Namave Industrial area.

Reporter:          A moment please, Sir. Which people will you employ to work in your company?

Chong Lee:      I will employ both Ugandans and Chinese.

Reporter:          Thank you very much, Sir. I wish you the best in Uganda.

Chong Lee:      It’s indeed my pleasure.
