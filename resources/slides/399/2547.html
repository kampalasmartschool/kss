Hardly had……/scarcely/barely had/No sooner had

When a story is told in the past tense, the adverbials hardly, scarcely, barely and no sooner are often used to emphasis that one event quickly followed by another. The verb describing the earlier event is usually in the past perfect tense.

Note:

        Hardly, scarcely and barely are followed by when, while no sooner s followed by than.

Examples

1. Musa stood up immediately the bell rang.

  • Hardly had the bell rung when Musa stood up.
  • Scarcely had the bell rung when Musa stood up.
  1. Hardly had I arrived home when my phone rang.
  2. Scarcely had she finished reading when she fell asleep.
  3. Barely had they won a match when the coach had a heart attack.
