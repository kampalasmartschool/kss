Differences between bacteria and fungi.

Fungi

Bacteria

Reproduce by means of spores

Reproduce by binary fusion.

They are big.

They are single celled.

Fungal diseases are not immunisable.

Bacterial diseases are immunisable.

Some are eaten as food.

No bacteria are eaten as food.

Fungal diseases are spread through close body contact.

Bacterial disease are spread through air, water and insects.

Ways of controlling fungal and bacterial diseases.

*Heat

Strong heat kills germs. This is why:-

  1. We boil drinking water.
  2. We iron clothes.
  3. We cook food before eating.
  4. We boil milk before drinking.
  5. Some medical equipment are sterilized by steaming or boiling.
