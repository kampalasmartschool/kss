FUELS.

A fuel is anything that burns to produce heat and light energy.

Examples of fuels.

  • Fire wood.
  • Charcoal.
  • Oil (Petroleum)
  • Coal

Note:

  1. Fire wood and charcoal are renewable resources while oil and coal are non-renewable resources.
  2. Coal was formed from marshy vegetables and plants which lived long ago and were buried under ground but due to heat and pressure they changed to coal.
  3. Coal is burnt to get thermal electricity.
  4. Oil (petroleum) is refined through the process of fractional distillation.
  5. The products after refining crude oil (petroleum) are; Petrol, diesel and kerosene.
  6. These products are burnt to produce heat and light.
  7. Petrol and diesel are used to run engines.
  8. Oil was formed from animals which lived long ago and were buried due to heat and pressure they changed to oil.
