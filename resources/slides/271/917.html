POULTRY DISEASES.

a) Coccidiosis:

Caused by a protozoa and it attacks the liver, small intestines and large intestines.

Signs of coccidiosis.

  • Blood stained droppings/ diarrhoea.
  • Ruffied feathers.
  • Dullness and dropping of wings.

Prevention of coccidiosis.

  • Use coccidiostats in feeds.
  • Cull and slaughter the infected ones.
  • Provide clean feeds in clean troughs.

b) Fowl Pox.

Caused by a virus spread through feather and skin pecking.

Signs of Fowl Pox.

  • Tinny wounds on comb wattle and wings.
  • Ulcers in the mouth.
  • Excessive discharge of fluids from eyes and nostrils.
  • Difficult breathing.

Prevention of fowl pox.

  • Routine vaccination and strict hygiene.
  • Practice good hygiene.
  • Cull and slaughter the infected.
  • Wash and disinfect the poultry house.

c) Newcastle Disease.

Caused by a Virus.

Signs of Newcastle Disease.

  • Greenish yellowish diarrhoea.
  • Staggering, dropping of wings and bending of neck.
  • Molting of feathers.
  • Poor egg shell formation.
  • Lameness and difficult breathing.

Prevention of Newcastle Disease.

  • Vaccinate the birds regularly
  • Cull slaughter and bury in infected.

d) Fowl Typhoid.

Caused by bacteria.

Signs of Fowl Typhoid.

  • White yellowish or green droppings.
  • Rough feathers.
  • Sleepy eyes.

e) Anaemia.

Combs and wattles get shrunken and pale yellow.

Prevention of Anaemia.

  • Vaccinate regularly.
  • Keep poultry house clean dry and well ventilated.
  • Cull and slaughter the infected ones.
