Dialogue

USING A DICTIONARY

Amanda:           Good afternoon, Natasha.

Natasha:           Good afternoon, Amanda.

Amanda:           Will you lend me your dictionary, please?

Natasha:           Sorry, Amanda. I am using it. Wait a minute or two.

Amanda:           Okay please.

Natasha:           Here it is! What is puzzling your brain?

Amanda:           A new word. I have failed to understand what it means.

Natasha:           Let’s look it up in the dictionary. Which word exactly?

Amanda:           “Boon companion”

Natasha:           Wow! It is very strange to me, too.

Amanda:           I have seen it, Natasha. It means “a very good friend.”

Natasha:           You mean you are mu boon companion, Amanda?

Amanda:           We should learn to check up words in a dictionary. It will even help us to acquire more vocabulary, improve on spelling but above all to avoid                            being a laughing stock.

Natasha:           Do you know how to pronounce the word ‘ewe’.

Amanda:           No, I don’t.

Natasha:           Don’t worry. It is ‘you’. A dictionary can as well help you to improve your pronunciation and articulation of words.

Amanda:           Which of these words come before the other in the dictionary, bin or bean?

Natasha:           “Bean” comes before “bin” in the dictionary because it has ‘e’ in the second position whereas ‘bin’ has    “i”
