Necessity and obligation

Obligation is the must to do something.

Necessity is the fact that something must happen or done or the need for something

The following words are used to express necessity and obligation e.g. need, must, needn’t, mustn’t etc

Note: take note of the changes of these verbs

Present                         past

Needn’t                         didn’t need to/ needn’t have

Must                             had to / was / were to

Mustn’t                          didn’t have to/ wasn’t to

 

Use of must

It is used when something is necessary e.g.

  • She must accompany me.
  • We must go and see him.

The past form of must is had to e.g.

  • She had to accompany me
  • We had to go and see him
