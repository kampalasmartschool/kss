PRONOUNS

A pronoun is a word that can be used in a place of a noun.

KINDS OF PRONOUNS

Lower class work .Replacing the underlined nouns with pronouns in sentences.

  • Personal pronouns e.g. I, we, she, he, it, you
  • Possessive pronouns e.g. mine, yours, ours, hers, his, their, it’s
  • Adjectives pronouns e.g. their, your, my, our, her, his.
  • Reflex pronouns e.g. yourself, herself, himself, themselves, ourselves, itself, oneself, myself.
  • Demonstrative pronouns e.g. this, these, that, those.
  • Reflexive pronouns e.g. when, who, which, that, why, whom, whose.

Personal pronouns (subjective or nominative pronoun)

  1. I came yesterday.
  2. You are my chief enemy.
  3. He fasted for two days.
  4. She is absent.
  5. It was on TV.
  6. We will go together.
  7. They promised to come today

Objective pronouns

Examples

  1.  They will eat with us.
  2. Give that book to me.
  3. She works with him.
  4. I shall give it to her.
  5. She stays with them.
