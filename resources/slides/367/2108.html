PARTS OF AN INFORMAL LETTER

1. The writer’s address e.g.

  • Springfield Academy

       P.O Box 25629,

      Kampala

2. Date e.g.

  • 21st February, 2015
  • 21/2/2015

3. Greetings/ salutation e.g.

  • Hello cousin
  • Dear Mum
  • Dear Joseph

4. Introduction; body Message

The body contains the message the write intends to send to the receiver.

Examples

I hope you are fine. I am just writing to say happy birthday to you

Thank you very much for our letter which I received last week.

I am writing to invite you to my birthday party which will take place next week.

5. Conclusion /ending e.g.

  • Your loving ….
  • Yours sincerely
  • Your loving cousin
  • Your best friend
  • Yours affectionately, etc

6. The writer’s name and signature e.g.

  • John Mullet
  • Taj Kalyango 

ACTIVITY

Using your school address, write a letter to your best friend congratulating him/her upon his/her election as the new head boy/girl of her school. Use between 200-250 words.
