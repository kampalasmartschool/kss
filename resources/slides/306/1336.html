QUESTION TAGS IN PRESENT SIMPLE TENSE

A question tag is a short sentence that comes at the end of the statement. We use it to turn a statement into a question.

Rules of question tags.

  • When the sentence is affirmative/positive, the tag changes to negative.
  • When the sentence is negative, the tag must be affirmative/positive question tag consists of helping verbs such as can, do, will, must, have, has, etc and pronouns such as I, he, she, they, we it, etc.

Examples

  1. You are early, aren’t you?
  2. They don’t eat pork, do they?
  3. You can drive me, can’t you?
