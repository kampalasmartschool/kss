Ungulates (hoofed mammals).

All mammals in this group are herbivorous i.e. they feed on vegetation.

Ungulates are divided into two sub-groups:-

  1. Even toed ungulates
  2. Odd toed ungulates

a) Even toed ungulates.

They include; Okapi, deer, pigs, cattle, giraffe, sheep, antelopes, hippopotamus

Even toed ungulates are subdivided into two;

  1. Ruminants.
  2. Non ruminants.

i. Ruminants

These are ungulates with four stomachs and chew cuds. Most of them have horns which they use for protection.

Examples

  • Cattle.
  • Goats.
  • Sheep.
  • Camel.
  • Antelope etc.

ii. Non ruminants.

  • These are ungulates which do not chew cud but have a complete dentition.
  • They have well developed canines which they use for protection.

Examples include:

  • Pigs
  • Hippopotamus
  • Warthog.

b) Odd toed ungulates.

The number of toes is always odd.

Examples: one in horse, donkey, Zebra and three in elephant and Rhino.
