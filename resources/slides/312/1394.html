Insect eating mammals (insectivores).

  • These are mammals which feed on insects.
  • They have a sensitive snout and strong claws for digging.
  • They are nocturnal i.e. they hunt at night and sleep during day.
  • Examples include; Hedgehog, elephant shrew, Aardvark.
  • A hedgehog is an animal with spines on its body. When threatened, it rolls itself up into a ball.

 A hedgehog

Flying mammals (chiroptera).

Bats are the only true flying mammals.

They have a fold skin attached to the fore limb which act as wings.

There are three main types of bats:

  1. Insect eating bats: These are helpful to man because they eat many harmful insects like mosquitoes.
  2. Fruit eating bats
  3. Blood sucking bats (vampires): They suck blood from large animals like horses, cows, buffaloes etc. bats are nocturnal i.e. they hunt and sleep during day. They can find their food at night in complete darkness by using echoes from the sound they produce.

Pouched mammals (marsupials).

Examples of these include; Kangaroo, Koala bear and Wallabies

Marsupials are found in Australia and South America.

A kangaroo

Egg laying mammals (monotremes).

These are the most primitive in the class of mammals because:

  • They resemble birds and reptiles in that they lay eggs and also have beaks similar to those of birds.
  • They have characteristics of birds, mammals and reptiles.
  • Monotremes are regarded as mammals because they feed their young ones on milk from the mammary glands and for the care they give to their young ones after hatching.
  • Examples include; the duck billed platypus and the spiny ant eater (echidna) of Australia

 A duck billed platypus.

Sea mammals (cetaceans).

  • These are mammals which live in the sea.
  • They don’t have gills but they breathe by means of lungs
  • They have a layer of fats under the skin called blubber to keep them warm.
  • They have a high level of intelligence next to primates
  • They have mammary glands.
  • They give birth to living young ones and suckle them on milk from the mammary glands.
  • Most sea mammals have fur.

Examples include; Blue whale, Sperm whale, porpoises and seals
