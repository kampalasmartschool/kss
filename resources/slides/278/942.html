THE DIGESTIVE SYSTEM.

Types of digestion.

a) Physical  (Mechanical).

This type is carried out by the action of teeth and tongue.

It takes place in the mouth.

b) Chemical digestion.

It is carried out by the action of the enzymes.

It starts from the mouth to ileum.

ENZYMES.

Enzymes are chemical substances that speed up food digestion.

Examples of enzymes.

Salivary amylase (ptylin), pepsin, rennin, lipase, trypsin, panaeatic amylase.

Site

Digestive juice

Enzyme

Mouth

 Saliva

Salivary amylase

Stomach

 Gastric juice

Pepsin and Renin

Duodenum

 Pancreatic juice

Lipase, trypsin, pancreatic amylase

 Bile

No enzymes

NB: The ileum produces intestinal juice with enzymes that completes the digestion of food.

Characteristics of enzymes.

  • They are proteins in nature.
  • They are destroyed by high temperature.
  • They work on specific classes (type) of food.
  • They work under conditions ie acidic or alkaline.

Example of enzymes under each condition.

Alkaline.

  • Amylase.
  • Lipase.
  • Trypsin.

Acidic.

  • Pepsin.
  • Rennin (only in breast feeding babies).
