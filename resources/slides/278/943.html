FOOD IN THE MOUTH.

Food is broken down into smaller particles through chewing. Saliva is mixed with the food to soften and lubricate it for easy swallowing. Chewed food is rolled into bolus by the tongue and pushed into the gullet.

Saliva is produced by the salivary glands. It contains an enzyme called salivary amylase or ptyalin which begins digesting cooked starch in the mouth.

During swallowing, the epigilottis closes over the opening of the trachea to prevent food from entering into it.

a) Roles of saliva during digestion.

  • It softens and moistens food.
  • It cools down food in case it is hot.

b)  Roles of the tongue during digestion.

  • It mixes food with saliva.
  • It rolls food into a bolus.
  • It pushes food into the gullet.

c)   Roles of the teeth during digestion.

  • To break down food into small particles. (They aid mechanical digestion)
  • It protects the tongue.
